Work

My first real job experience came my freshman year of college working at the National Institute for Aviation Research. In the Spring of 2019, I began as a mechanical tester of Composite Laminate Specimens utilizing test methods provided by ASTM. I worked on projects for companies such as Spirit Aerosystems, Boeing, NASA, and more.

After about 18 months as a tester, an opportunity arose for me to transition groups to learn automation. This transition was extremely formative for me as I learned to use industry-standard automation equipment like Siemens Programmable Logic Controllers as well as Kuka robots. I was also exposed to and worked with various aircraft composite inspection methods such as Thermography, Shearography and Laser Paint Removal. Rapid prototyping and developent, leadership, task dissemination, teamwork, and maintaining professional relationships with our vendors were crucial skills I learned on top of my technical training.

While in this position I was regarded highly on my project team, facilitating meetings with vendors and being given the opportunity to travel to Corpus Christi Texas to assist in installing our project. However, after 21 months on the project, I decided that it was time for me to further my Cyber Security Career, and left to pursue an internship at Koch Industries as a Cyber Security Architect Inter. I am grateful for the time I spent at NIAR and the relationships I was able to create with some of the fantastic people there.

Professional Projects

While working as an Automation Engineering Student Intern at the National Institute for Aviation Research, I was priviliged to work on an exciting project known as JARVIS (Joint Autonomous Repair Verification and Inspection System). This project alone taught me so much information, and has given me so many skills I can translate into my career and personal ventures. For example, I now have knowledge of low-voltage DC circuits such as 24V and 12V, as well as high-voltage AC circuits including 120V, 220V, and 480V while being able to select appropriate wire gauge, terminate connections, and solder connectors. Also, I have learned skills in Automation and SCADA systems, such as being able to program in Siemens' TIA Portal v16+, WorkVisual for Kuka KRL, Keyence Safety Device configurator, and proper configuration of an airgapped Local Area Network.

This project has also taught me fabrication skills, specifically working with Aluminum and Steel sheets of 1/8", 1/4", and 1/2" thicknesses, drilling, tapping, and painting. But most importantly, this project has taught me the importance of teamwork and communication. I have been fortunate to work with some highly qualified, intelligent and respectful supervisors on this project, but I have also had my fair dealings of people that are difficult to deal with. Learning how to handle, motivate, and communicate with those individuals truly has helped me build a strong career foundation.

Kali Linux Projects

Also in my classes, I have learned basic functionality of Kali Linux, as well as its usefulness for Penetration Testing of Web Applications. On my Kali Machine at home, I host OWASP Juice Shop, a Capture-The-Flag style web application that is intentionally insecure to train up and coming cybersecurity professionals. While working on Kali and Juice Shop, I have learned how to execute simple SQL Injections, carry out brute force attacks using BurpSuite, access hidden files such as FTPs to gain access to .json package lists (then find the vulnerabilities of those packages), and write penetration testing reports with clear methodology and professionalism to give to the customer.
